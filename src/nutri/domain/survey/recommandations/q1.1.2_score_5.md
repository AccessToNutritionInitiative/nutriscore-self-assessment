question_text: "Is providing healthy food part of your business plan?"
score: "5"
---

We recommend your foods and beverages are formulated to address the main malnutrition challenges in your country or among the customers you aim to serve. This could be by making sure they are low in fat or salt or sugar if the main challenge is overweight and obesity, or high blood pressure and diabetes. Or it might be that your customers are anaemic (lack iron in their diets) or lack other key vitamins and minerals (micronutrients); you can help them by adding these micronutrients to your products. Formulating your products to help them manage their health may help to increase consumer demand.

Learn more about how the World Health Organization (WHO) defines a ‘Healthy Diet’ here: <https://www.who.int/docs/default-source/healthy-diet/healthy-diet-fact-sheet-394.pdf?sfvrsn=69f1f9a1_2&download=trued> .

For more information, look at the websites of GAINhealth.org and the NutritionConnect.org platform. to see the business case for investing in better nutrition, including its impact on productivity, the case for a healthier workforce, and the case for brand integrity, as well as the ethical case.
