question_text: "Do you use any criteria to define healthy, nutritious products?"

Defining what's nutritious and healthy is an important step for your business. It is important to use criteria developed by experts or government agencies, rather than set them yourself. Check that your criteria align to expert advice. Many countries rely on WHO and FAO (Food and Agriculture Organization) to establish and share information on dietary recommendations and nutritional requirements. You can find more information here: <https://www.who.int/nutrition/topics/nutrecomm/en/>

 The ‘gold standard’ is to classify or rank foods according to their nutritional composition, i.e., 'Nutrient Profiling'. Several nutrient profiling tools are available for this: for example
• The Health Star Rating model (HSR):
<http://www.healthstarrating.gov.au/internet/healthstarrating/publishing.nsf/content/home>
• The NUTRISCORE model: <https://nutriscore.colruytgroup.com/colruytgroup/en/about-nutri-score/>.
• Other nutrient profiling models such as the WHO Euro model, can be used to assess product healthiness to determine which products can be marketed to children: <https://www.euro.who.int/__data/assets/pdf_file/0005/270716/Nutrient-children_web-new.pdf>

Nutrient profiling models can also be used to determine whether a product can carry a health or nutrition claim or whether foods can be provided to public institutions. In some countries – which you might export to - they are used to decide whether a ‘healthy’ logo or symbol can be used on the pack. They can also be useful in informing and educating your customers.
