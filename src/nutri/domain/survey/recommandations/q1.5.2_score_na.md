Different businesses have certifications for different reasons: for example, a product that is awarded a certified quality mark informs suppliers and consumers that the product is of high quality. It is a common “condition of trade” or trade law that food business and food manufacturers have third-party certification. Some certifications are mandatory or a legal national/ international requirement for sale within country or exporting products abroad. Proactive food businesses obtain certifications to ensure that all possible food safety risks are covered – which makes good and logical business sense. Obtaining certifications will enable exportation of your products to other markets that require or give preference to certification.
